Speakers

Senior figures from public-sector agencies, universities, and the private space industry, sharing what they are working on and what they expect from the next decade.

AK

Dr. Asma Khan

Director General, SUPARCO

Dr. Khan leads strategic planning at SUPARCO and has overseen Pakistan’s recent transition toward a commercial-friendly space programme. Her keynote opens the conference with the national roadmap.

TM

Dr. Tariq Mansoor

Chief Engineer, Launch Systems, SUPARCO

Dr. Mansoor heads the launch vehicle programme at SUPARCO and previously led several satellite payload integration projects. His Day 3 keynote covers what a domestic launch capability looks like in practice.

SM

Prof. Sadia Mahmood

Earth Observation Lab, NUST

Professor Mahmood’s lab uses satellite imagery for climate and disaster work across South Asia. She presents a session on Earth observation applied to floods, glacier monitoring, and agricultural yield forecasting.

IS

Imran Sheikh

Founder & CEO, Astralis Group

Imran is the founder of Astralis Group, an Islamabad-based aerospace company working on space-tourism partnerships. His talk argues for realistic timelines and customer-grade safety standards.

AT

Barrister Ayesha Tariq

Space & Aviation Law, Tariq Chambers

A practising barrister specialising in international space law, the ITU regime, and frequency coordination. Her keynote on Day 2 walks operators through the legal scaffolding any new space business needs.

BA

Dr. Bilal Ahmad

Senior Researcher, IST Aerospace Lab

Dr. Ahmad’s recent work focuses on running machine-learning inference on small-satellite hardware. His talk surveys the practical limits of onboard AI and where the field is heading.

MY

Maria Yusuf

Geospatial Engineer, Independent

Maria runs satellite-image processing workshops for the GIS community in South Asia and has worked on remote-sensing projects with the UN and the World Bank. She leads a hands-on Python workshop on Day 2.

HR

Hassan Raza

Editor, South Asia Space Review

Hassan covers the regional space industry as a journalist and analyst, and moderates the Day 1 commercial-launch panel. He is widely cited on Pakistan’s space policy debates.

SM

Engr. Saad Mehmood

Frequency Coordination Specialist

Saad has worked on frequency-coordination filings to the ITU for Pakistani satellite operators since 2014. His Day 2 talk explains the timelines and traps that catch out new operators.

Plus 25+ workshop leads, panel guests, and student presenters across the three days.
